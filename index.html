Our Teaching Method

From Zero to Engineer in 4 Steps

Every project in this boot camp — from a simple night light to an AI-powered irrigation system — is built using the same professional engineering workflow. Students don't just follow instructions; they learn how real robotics teams think.

01

1. Plan the Project

Before touching any wires, students define the problem they're solving and what a successful project should do — just like a real engineering team.

02

2. Select Equipment

Students learn to choose the right microcontroller, sensors, motors, and components for the job — and understand exactly why each part matters.

03

3. Design the Circuit

Students sketch and read circuit diagrams, learning how power and signals flow between components before building anything.

04

4. Build, Code & Test

Finally, students assemble the hardware, write the program, then test, debug, and improve it — completing a real, working project.

The Journey

5-Day Curriculum Roadmap

A sequence of 4 real projects — each one building on the last — gives parents and instructors a clear, glanceable overview of what the young innovators will plan, build, and showcase every day.

Day 01

The Spark: Project Planning & Electronics Foundation

Project Core

The Smart Night Light

  • Kick-off: introducing the Plan → Select → Design → Build workflow used for every project this week.
  • Introduction to microcontrollers, breadboards, and circuit mapping with resistors and LEDs.
  • Selecting and wiring an LDR (light sensor) to detect real-time brightness levels.
  • Programming the microcontroller to automatically switch a light on when it gets dark.
Technical Pillar: Engineering Workflow & Basic Circuitry
Parents' Corner Skill: Inventory Management
Day 02

Robotics & Motor Control: Build Your RC Car

Project Core

The RC Car

  • Planning the build: choosing a chassis, wheels, and the right motor driver for an RC car.
  • Understanding DC motors, power delivery, and voltage regulation.
  • Designing the wiring diagram and connecting an L298N/L293D motor driver.
  • Assembling and programming a 2WD/4WD remote-controlled robotic car from scratch.
Technical Pillar: Mechanical Assembly & Motor Control
Parents' Corner Skill: Workspace Etiquette
Day 03

Intelligent Navigation: The Obstacle-Avoiding Car

Project Core

The Smart Obstacle-Avoiding Car

  • Upgrading yesterday's RC car: planning and selecting an ultrasonic sensor and a servo motor.
  • Redesigning the circuit to integrate ultrasonic sonar for measuring distance to obstacles.
  • Calibrating the servo motor to scan the surroundings dynamically.
  • Writing nested If-Else logic so the car detects and avoids obstacles completely on its own.
Technical Pillar: Autonomous Navigation & Logic Building
Parents' Corner Skill: Failure Analysis & Debugging
Day 04

IoT & AI: Smart Irrigation System

Project Core

IoT Smart Irrigation System + AI Prediction

  • Introduction to IoT: connecting microcontrollers to Wi-Fi networks.
  • Interfacing a soil moisture sensor with a relay to automatically control a water pump.
  • Adding a simple AI feature that predicts when the irrigation system should turn on or off.
  • Workshop: how to structure and design a project presentation.
Technical Pillar: IoT, Automation & Applied AI
Parents' Corner Skill: Presentation Skills & Public Speaking
Day 05

Grand Innovation Challenge & Awards Day

Deliverable Core

Final Presentations, Competition & Certificates

  • Every student presents their week's project live in front of judges, parents, and peers.
  • Live competition: cars and projects are demonstrated and judged on creativity, function, and presentation.
  • The top 2 innovators are awarded prizes for the best projects.
  • Every participant receives an official Certificate of Participation.
Technical Pillar: End-to-End Project Delivery
Parents' Corner Skill: Confidence, Leadership & Sportsmanship

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my child need any prior coding or electronics experience?

Not at all. This boot camp is designed to start from absolute zero level. Students begin with the fundamentals of microcontrollers and basic circuits on Day 1, then build up to robotics, IoT, and AI step by step across the 5 days.

What will my child actually build during the camp?

Over 5 days, students build a sequence of 4 real projects: a Smart Night Light (Day 1), an RC Car (Day 2), an upgraded Obstacle-Avoiding Car (Day 3), and an AI-powered IoT Smart Irrigation System (Day 4) — then present all of it on Competition Day (Day 5).

What teaching approach do you use?

Every project follows the same 4-step engineering workflow: Plan the project, Select the right equipment, Design the circuit, then Build, Code & Test. The program is 80% hands-on practical work and 20% logic/theory.

How many students are in each batch?

Each slot is strictly limited to 10 students, so every child gets individual attention and hands-on time with the equipment for every project.

Will my child receive a certificate or win anything?

Yes. On the final day, every student presents their project in a live competition. The top 2 innovators win prizes, and every student who completes the program receives an official Certificate of Participation.
